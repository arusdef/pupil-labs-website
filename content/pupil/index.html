Pupil is in active, community driven development. {% endblock headline %} {% block overview %} ## Overview **Pupil** is a head mounted mobile eye tracking platform. With **Pupil**, you will be able to record gaze positions. This means that you can view what someone else is looking at in real time, record gaze positions, and visualize results. Pupil software is [Open Source](http://github.com/pupil-labs/pupil "Pupil Github"). You can customize the software to your needs, build your own plugins, and contribute back to the community! {% endblock %} {% block hardware %} ## Hardware Pupil headsets are 3D printed frames that you wear like a pair of glasses. A lot of design and engineering energy has gone into getting the ergonomics of the headset just right. Learn More - Pupil Hardware Design Pupil headsets hold two cameras. One camera tracks and records your eye movements, and another camera records your field of view. Pupil headsets are lightweight and compatible with most modern computers with 2 USB ports. Pupil headsets currently come in two flavors - **Pupil** and **Pupil Pro**. {% endblock %} {% block price_table %}

Pupil is 3D printed pre-tensioned.

When you put on the Pupil headset, it will transform into the shape we designed.



Finite Element Analysis

The video is a visualization of the Fininte Element Analysis simulation that we use to compute the exact geometry of the headset. We print the deformed geometry. When you put the headset on your head, cameras come into alignment and the headset fits securely.

Features

FEA simulations allow us to design a headset that performs robustly and embodies all qualities we find important in a device that you will be wearing.

  • Thin
  • Lightweight
  • Stable - doesn't shift around while wearing
  • Comfortable to Wear
  • Unobtrusive
